So run—skate across the ice, drive toward love,
chase the joy you believe is yours to keep.

But when they call it unintentional,
what they really mean is unattended.

Tomorrow, will you ration care?
Tomorrow, will you still refuse to ration death?

The waiting rooms will still be full,
lined with ghosts before they’ve turned to ash.
A bloodied hand will reach toward you,
fingers curled in the shape of a plea,
desperate to hold, to love, to stay.
