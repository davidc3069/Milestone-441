To my mirage of tomorrow,
The numbers are in.

We clung to the promise of a new millennium,
brushed away the brittle dust of the past,
smiled wide, teeth bared against the unknown,
waved our history goodbye, and our futures—hello.

Somewhere outside, someone leaves a note.
Somewhere outside, a phone rings into silence.
A soul, somewhere out there, can no longer pretend.
But alas, we’ll focus on tomorrow.

But what does tomorrow really promise?
Is she softer, sweeter,
Does she glimmer in the early light like fool’s gold?

Does she hush the mother in the ward,
rocking prayers between trembling fingers,
watching, waiting, whispering spells of survival?

Does she quiet the humming of the machines,
or does she feed them?
Does she dispense the poison, or does she supply it?

Tomorrow, the stock price rises.

But don’t shy away from this horror now.
We’ve only just begun.
